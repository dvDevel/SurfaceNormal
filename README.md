# SurfaceNormal
This repository is used to describe the normal (surface normal).

Normal that goes straight out from the surface of a sphere at the point of contact, forming a 90° angle with the surface (or with the tangent plane/rectangle touching it).

The mathematical term for that direction is:

Normal (or Surface Normal)

A normal is a line or vector that is perpendicular (90°) to a surface at a given point.

For a sphere, the normal direction at any point is the line that goes from that point toward the center of the sphere.

Key relationships:

The rectangle touching the sphere is called a tangent plane.

The line that goes 90° upward from that rectangle is the normal vector.

On a sphere, that normal always points toward the center.

So the structure is:

Sphere surface

Tangent plane (your rectangle touching the sphere)

Normal vector (90° direction from that plane)

Natural way to ask this in English

Your sentence:

“What is the mathematical term a direction from touch point a sphere and rectangle to 90 degree up side?”

A more natural version would be:

“What is the mathematical term for the direction perpendicular to a sphere at the point of contact with a plane?”

or simpler:

“What do we call the direction perpendicular to the surface of a sphere at a point?”

Why this is more natural:

English normally uses “perpendicular to” instead of “90 degree up side.”

“Point of contact” is the standard phrase for touching surfaces.

✅ Answer: The direction is called the normal (or normal vector).
